Canopy coefficients for the clean-wing archetypes (Mk I, Mk III) are anchored
to the open TU Delft V3 LEI benchmark (awegroup/TUDELFT_V3_KITE, CC-BY):

- **Wind tunnel** (Poland et al., WES 11, 911, 2026): 1:6.5 rigid model of the
  25 m² V3, Re 5e5. CL_max ≈ 1.07 @ 18°; clean-wing (L/D)_max ≈ 8.7 @ 9°.
- **CFD RANS** (Viré et al., Energies 15, 1450, 2022): with-strut, Re 1e6.
  CL_max ≈ 1.35 @ 19°.

The V3's projected aspect ratio (3.498) is close to Mk I's (~3.6), so the
polar transfers with less shape penalty than usual. A bridle parasitic-drag
term (bluff-body lines, scaled from the V3's 82-line / 96 m system by area^0.5)
is added to the clean-wing polar to form a *system* polar.

Result: the hand-picked cl_op = 0.8 / cd_op = 0.15 used for Mk I sit within
15% (resultant-force coefficient) of the benchmark operating point
(CL 0.8 reached at α ≈ 8°, clean cd ≈ 0.10, system cd ≈ 0.10–0.15 depending
on bridle assumptions). The benchmark **validates** the first-pass numbers
rather than moving them — so the tow-force and wind-envelope figures above
now carry a citable source.

Caveats: Mk II's lobe-degraded wing and Mk IV (no wing) are *not* covered by
this benchmark. And the V3 is a crosswind-optimised AWE kite; our static
lifters never see its crosswind apparent-wind multiplier, which is why our
per-m² tow loads are lower than the V3's 5.8 kN nominal (that force is at
crosswind speed ~3× wind, not true wind).
